Management Summary



Executive Team- 



Rick Banks

Rick Banks is a Milwaukee native who is joining BLOC after spending three years as a neighborhood organizer. He’s been an activist and an organizer in Milwaukee since 2010 when he was spurred by protests following the passage of ACT 10. Rick proudly identifies as pro-labor, pro-student rights, pro-women & LGBT+ equality, and he strongly opposes white supremacy and police violence.



Paul Wellington





Paul Wellington holds a Master’s in Architecture from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, though he works as a public library supervisor. He is the author of Black Built: History and Architecture in the Black Community, a publication discussing the work of Black architects in the United States. Paul also runs the international blog ArchBrick Daily, showcasing the fan made LEGO architecture. 





Ayrton Bryan





Ayrton is a recent transplant to Milwaukee, but has already grown to love and value the many faces of the city. With a Bachelor’s from the south and Master’s from the east, he has found himself instantly welcomed by the warm MKE community and wanting to give back. He works as an Environmental Health and Safety Manager. He is an avid (audiobook) reader, yoga-fan, craft beer junkie, martial arts student and mindfulness practitioner.





OPERATIONS



Summary



Black City Collective’s business model is to provide a mobile app software that we offer to white-label to other organizations on a regionally exclusive basis.  The primary use of the mobile app will be to provide a database a black-owned businesses, facilitate sales and advertisements, and the exchange of local digital and cryptocurrencies.   Our primary targeted market is organizers and organizations that seek to support Black-owned businesses.  Through partnering organizations, our secondary target markets are consumers who want to support Black-owned businesses.   By exporting branding and data collection to third party agencies BCC can quickly cover the most ground and build strong local nodes of Black owned Businesses that will contribute to a national database.  It also provides local organizations with an opportunity for branding.



MKE Black Incorporated, being the organization to launch the proto-type, shall receive a perpetual license to operate BLK City Collective’s app at the cost of $1/year.



Mobile App/ Directory





The main service that MKE Black is its MKE Black Mobile App. This mobile app, available on Android and Apple, will serve as the central database of black owned businesses in Milwaukee. This app will feature not just restaurant and retail locations but also local black vendors and online businesses.

Market Analysis:







As an economic development tool, supporting Black businesses is currently the best way to build wealth and income in black communities. The majority of job creation occurs at the small business level. Many small business suffer from lack of advertising. There currently isn’t a single place to ide A central place to find local black business.

There’s clearly a demand to support Black businesses and culture in both America and in Milwaukee.



Locally, Lilo Allen, a co-owner of The Bronzeville Collective, organized a “Brunching Makes You Black” event to support Coffee Makes You Black, a local black owned business, after new arise that they were struggling due to a long construction project. That event drew over 2000 responses on Facebook and drew hundreds of customers to the restaurant, including the local Alderman, during the two day event. 



In addition to local events, Milwaukee is home to several organizations and institutions that teach Black history, namely the Wisconsin Black Historical Society and American’s Black Holocaust Museum:





Wisconsin Black Historical Society opened in







America’s Black Holocaust Museum will also open sometime in the future.







MKE Black will aim to, collaborate with all of the above institutions to for mutual benefit and many will be featured on MKE Black Bus Tours.





Comparable Entities



Currently no other entity is doing exactly what MKE Black is doing: promoting black business, culture and history of Milwaukee through social media & bus tours. There are other organizations that promote specific black businesses, but none that showcase business and culture.



Businesses it could disrupt:

All

As recently as 2018, www.mkeblackbusiness.com attempted to have a database of black businesses. However, they attempted to run it as a for profit, gaining revenue from ads. Ultimately they catalogue was not very thorough and the website wasn’t very user friendly. Without the catalogue and traffic to support ads, it ultimately failed.





Our closest current “competitors” would be BlankSpaceMKE, an urban art cooperative with a very basic principle of spreading the message about cooperative economics – build people to build unity to build wealth to build power. Marketing Plan







MKE Black will do things differently by mixing in person events and tours to promote the use of the mobile app, further supporting black businesses and establishing a culture of supporting Black-owned businesses.



A staple of our marketing strategy will be “collaborative marketing” Unlike trade assosications that require a membership, MKE Black will let any black owned business have access to the resources, business research conversations, and connection network in exchange for marketing agreements. MKE Black will provide short, clear messages to companies to support and spread the word about MKE Black. By increasing our audience by connecting with the businesses we serve, MKE Black will be shown to more businesses and consumers than our marketing efforts alone could handle. 



To maintain membership in the MKE Black network and have access to resources, businesses will have minimum- “collaboration”  “technology”, and “support” points to meet , in efforts to promote cooperative economics, technological development, and community building.

FINANCIAL INFORMATION







Start-up costs:







MKE Black would require start-up funds for three phases:







Phase 1: MKE Black Mobile App



Phase 1 would see the original launch of the MKE Black mobile app in February 2020. Upfront costs for phase 1 includes the time to locate businesses and design the app, technical costs such as the subscription to BuildFire and vendor fees to access to app marketplaces such as Google Play and Apple Store.





Phase 2: MKE Black Operations Automation



Phase 2 would see the adoption of tools such as help scout, Customer Relationship Management Software, digital scheduling tools, and professional web tools. MKE Black anticipates spending $1500-$2000 annually on these softwares.





Phase 3: MKE Black Mobile App Upgrade







Phase 3 would see the mobile app upgrade to add the additional capacities detailed above. Upfront cost would be $20,000 to pay a coder to build the app. This would include creation of the app as well as teaching staff how to maintain it. Alternatively, additional funds could be

acquired to pay a coder to maintain the app. Launch of phase 3 would be contingent upon receiving/acquiring the funding.



Phase 4: MKE Black Grants

	Based on amount of funds raised, MKE Black would like to give back to black owned businesses, and the black community. Areas of focus will be education, to create future black entrepreneurs and professionals driven by a strong educational background.

Final Concepts 

Initial Concepts

The initial concept of the mobile application technology discussed with the stakeholders BLK City Collective & MKE Black was: create a white-label technology that contains functionality of the existing MKE Black app, while adding new features as prioritized stretch goals. The stretch goals are defined by importance: favorite businesses for users and a total favorite count for each business page, a rating for each business, and any additional API support (Google Table, Uber, Doordash, etc) that the stakeholders deem necessary. The technology must have a self-hosted directory that is shared with a web application. It must also have a submission form for review and approval.

Final Concepts

The surveys solidified the input stakeholders have shared, and their vision for the application did not change much. Our end-users would like intuitive ease of use and consistent user/business profile information. This means saving favorite locations and keeping up to date with the latest business updates of those locations. Ultimately, our minimum viable product is to create white-label mobile technology replicating the functionality of the MKE Black app with a self-hosted directory (meaning all the information about a business is viewable from within the app) and submission forms. The survey clarified the stretch goals are important and is what the end user desires other than the MVP. Our final concept does not change much thankfully due to the insight available from the iteration of the current MKE Black app.

Application Flowchart

The mobile application will be built with similar tabs and functions to the current mobile application with additional functionality. Things that remain the same are the different tabs that are seen on the homepage of the current application. The new functionality includes the customer profiles and log in ability, the ability to create favorites, the ability to share on social media, the ability to message or contact businesses directly with feedback, the ability to comment on business pages and the ability to have a favorites list. Additionally, the app will have an admin login since the stakeholders wanted a page to manage the businesses or look at applications from new businesses. 





Database Flowchart

The database flowchart below describes a basic overview for the database architecture and setup. Both the website and the mobile app will be connected to one Google Firebase Realtime Database. Within the Firebase Realtime database, the data will have a few sections: Customer Data, Admin Data, and Business Data. The Customer Data section will have Authentication information (login info) as well as a favorites list per user. The admin data will have authentication info for each admin user and data for submitted applications. The business data will contain info about the businesses, such as about info to be displayed, images, etc. On the other hand Google Firebase Realtime databases also have an area to define database rules. These rules encompass security features as well as defining user authentication. 



Figma Wireframe Designs

Login page

The logo is front and center, as it is the “cover” of the app that sets the color scheme.

Email/username and password entry fields, along with “Forgot password” and “Log in”, are the first buttons to appear—offer a positive experience for the end user who might often update the app or get logged out for other reasons.

Envelope/lock icons for email/password, respectively—visual indicators are important!

“Create an account” is next—big, inviting that a new user can’t miss.

The “or” between the login and account creation sections is important—without it, one might think to enter their information in the login email/password entry fields believing that they are in the process of creating their account, only to be met with more entry fiends after clicking “Create an account”. The “or” makes this separation clear.

Don’t care about liking and reviewing? “Continue as guest” is an option, which is great for out-of-towners who simply want to grab a quick bite to eat.

It’s the third option because it’s not ideal—we want to create frequent users. It’s not difficult to spot, however—we don’t want to prevent new users from finding the business they seek.

The header introduces the user and creates a personalized user experience.

Can switch between businesses and events via a bottom slider.

List of businesses

The businesses appear in an evenly-spaced list with a name and a quick visual—clicking on a specific business allows the user to see the address, hours, and description.

Businesses that are showcased on the MKE Black Application

These are business owners of locally owned Milwaukee businesses that are featured on MKE Black, and they would provide feedback on how they boost their businesses on the app. Furthermore, they can provide insight on what they would want to see in the enhanced BLK City Collective app in order to share their business information on the app.

2 - DATA COLLECTION

Share how you will be collecting data for customer insights and explain what the data collection process looks like for the product itself.

Collecting Data Process: Customer Survey and Business Survey

A group of individuals in the Milwaukee area were surveyed for their familiarity with the MKE Black application and website. Furthermore, after expressing their familiarity with MKE Black, the surveyed group was asked to describe what they would like to see in the enhanced website, BLK City Collective, a website not meant to replace MKE Black but to extend its goals to other communities outside of Milwaukee. The overall objective of this survey was to gain ideas of what potential customers desire for the BLK City Collective App as well as features and qualities necessary for it to succeed. In order to gain better insights on customer needs, potential customers identified which qualities (like interactivity, accuracy, diversity of businesses, etc) were most important in order for them to have the best experience with the website/app. In addition, the customers identified important features that they would like to see on the website such as the types of businesses promoted, the location of the business, and the products they provide. 

For the business survey, a group of businesses in the Milwaukee area were surveyed for the type of service they provide and if they are currently featured on the MKE Black application and website. Then, the businesses were asked to identify important features that the BLK City Collective website should provide in order for them to utilize it to promote their businesses. Also, businesses provided feedback on what information the BLK City Collective website should provide to the customers about their businesses as well.

Initial Concepts

Our initial concept was to create a website that was a platform for cities to create their own version of MKE Black, for their cities called BLK City Collective. We aim to provide a platform that allows local communities to be intentional in their business support. The main goal of the BLK City Collective website is to promote minority-owned businesses as well as provide a tool for a locally based entity to take up the work of collecting information and promoting the database. In other words, the general template of the BLK City Collective app and website will be sold to a group for a city to boost underrepresented communities and their businesses. Once the enterprises get added to the app and website they will be able to interact with the users of the project. The goals defined by importance respectively are favorite businesses and display total favorite count and a rating category. The technology must have a self-hosted directory that is shared with an IOS/Android app. It must also have a submission form for review and approval.

Final Concepts 

Our final concept for BLK City Collective is a new mobile app and website inspired by MKE Black to help amplify their goals. BLK City Collective is not meant to replace MKE Black, but it is enhanced to reach other areas outside of Milwaukee, or other non-profits in other cities to perform a similar function that MKE Black has been able to do in Milwaukee. The technology is meant to provide business information to include type of business, location, as well as a bio of the business itself. After collecting feedback from various Milwaukee residents and businesses from the community, we determined that the most important aspects of our application are: ease of use/accessibility and up to date/accurate information. There is also a sizable demand for a way to interact with each business that could be in the form of a ranking system (5-star rating scale or upvote/downvote), public-facing comments, or communicating directly with the business itself. After interacting with the businesses, the businesses themselves would want a way to get the customers contact information like emails, phone numbers, etc. Finally, both the businesses and consumers expressed a demand for a sort of coupon/discount feature as a way to entice people to use our platform over the competitors. For example, a customer that favorited a specific business or left positive feedback, would be eligible to receive discounts for that business.

Product and Customer Definition

Introduction to Product

<Describe the essence of what your product will do. This is your “elevator pitch” - you have 1 minute. 2 or 3 brief, to the point, sentences.> 

BLK City Collective is a new website inspired by MKE Black to help amplify their goals of increasing visibility to small and local black-owned businesses to promote investment, appreciation, and awareness for underrepresented communities outside of Milwaukee; however, it is not designed to replace MKE Black, but enhanced to reach other areas outside of Milwaukee to perform a similar function that MKE Black has done here in Milwaukee. Our product is to create a website to host BLK City Collective for the MKE Black Company, so they can distribute it as a framework to other cities across the US to bring representation of local minority owned businesses in each city. The BLK City Collective website will provide business information such as type of business, location, products, as well as the bio of the business itself, and it will also provide a site for community members to locate and interact with specific minority owned businesses in their respective geographic region. 



Description of Problem

<Describe what problem your product will solve. 1 to 2 paragraphs.> 

Across the world, there seems to be a glaring discrepancy of representation of people who typically are underrepresented in communities compared to those who are not. Furthermore, it is very hard for communities made up of black people, women, labor supporters, etc, to support themselves without access to the knowledge of where their colleagues are and a means of connection. The problem lies within these underrepresented groups because there is a lack of public voice, and a lack of understanding of how to help these segmented groups. The vision of BLK City Collective is to create communities that elevate traditionally underrepresented voices up to have representation in their respective communities. This product, the BLK City Collective website, will allow members of underrepresented communities to find these minority owned businesses and support them intentionally. BLK City Collective’s overall mission is to “Put the UNITY back in Community”, and its focus is to Build, Leverage, and Keep (BLK) interaction between minority owned (specifically Black owned) businesses and their local communities. 



Primary Customer

<Describe your primary (or Target) customer. 1 to 2 paragraphs.> 

The primary customers for BLK City Collective website is aimed towards young adults within underrepresented communities as well as minority or Black owned businesses that would like to have themselves advertised on the BLK City Collective website. Young adults who are interested in learning about and interacting with Black owned businesses will use this product to gain better insight on what the Black owned businesses are like in the community they’re currently in, and how to support them. For the current MKE Black application and website, young adults are the main target group, and similarly for the BLK City Collective website/application, young adults would be the ones who would primarily be utilizing it, so it is imperative that their needs and desires for an enhanced version of MKE Black directed towards other cities across the United States are met. 

In addition, black owned businesses would utilize the website as a means to advertise themselves and their businesses to users of the website. Businesses would be the major backbone of the website because in order for the website to be successful in boosting an inclusive community and creating representation, black owned businesses would need to be featured on the website. Without businesses to be featured on the BLK City Collective website, young adult users would not be able to interact with businesses and discover Black owned services. By creating an account for their business, businesses would be able to interact with community members around them as well as boost their services that they offer.
